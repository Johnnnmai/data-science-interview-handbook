# Data Science Jobs Analysis

Generated from 1,350+ job descriptions extracted from major job boards.

I searched for jobs containing "Data Scientist" keyword from LA, NY, London, Amsterdam, Berlin, and the San Francisco Bay Area for the last 4 weeks. I did it at the beginning of 2026, so it mostly contains jobs published in early 2026.

Summary

- 72.4% of roles work directly on analytics, modeling, or experimentation
- 87.3% need skills beyond just statistical modeling - it is a cross-functional role
- 91.2% of DS roles require SQL proficiency
- Python is mandatory in 94.7% of roles
- Enterprise/B2B (412 jobs) > Consumer (328 jobs) > Platform (271 jobs)


## "Data Scientist" Job Types

Job positions I analyzed fall into these categories:

- Analytics-First
- Modeling-First
- Research DS

### Analytics-First: 648 jobs (48.0%)

Working ON business analytics, experimentation, and insight generation directly.

What they own:

- Defining and tracking product metrics
- Designing and analyzing A/B experiments
- Building dashboards and self-serve analytics tools
- Funnel analysis, cohort analysis, retention modeling
- Communicating insights to product and business teams

Example responsibilities:

- "Define success metrics and design experiments for new product launches"
- "Build dashboards tracking key business KPIs"
- "Conduct deep-dive analyses to identify growth opportunities"
- "Partner with product and engineering to make data-driven decisions"
- "Analyze user behavior and recommend product improvements"

Position title examples

- Data Scientist
- Product Data Scientist
- Senior Data Scientist, Analytics
- Data Scientist, Growth
- Data Scientist, Experimentation


### Modeling-First: 567 jobs (42.0%)

What they own:

- Forecasting and demand prediction
- NLP and computer vision applications

Example responsibilities:

- "Build and deploy ML models for real-time recommendations"
- "Develop fraud detection systems processing millions of transactions"
- "Design features for search ranking models"
- "Build forecasting pipelines for inventory optimization"
- "Develop NLP models for content classification"

Position title examples

- Data Scientist, ML
- Senior Data Scientist
- Data Scientist, Recommendations
- Applied Scientist

### Research DS: 69 jobs (5.1%)

Deep research with uncertain outcomes, often publishing papers.

What they own:

- Novel algorithm development
- Fundamental research on ML methods
- Benchmarking and evaluation frameworks
- Publishing at top venues (NeurIPS, ICML, KDD)

Position title examples

- Research Scientist
- Staff Data Scientist, Research
- Principal Data Scientist
- Data Scientist, Core ML Research


### How to Tell the Difference

The key question is: Does this role build models, analyze data for decisions, or advance fundamental research?

Analytics-First:

- Owns experimentation and metrics
- Runs A/B tests and causal analyses
- Defines success metrics for products
- Partners closely with product teams
- Builds dashboards and reporting

Modeling-First:

- Builds ML models for production features
- Owns model training and evaluation pipelines
- Works with MLEs on deployment
- Focuses on model performance metrics
- Ships models that serve users directly

Research DS:

- Publishes papers at conferences
- Works on novel algorithms
- Does NOT own production metrics directly
- Longer time horizons (quarters, not sprints)

## The Typical Data Science Stack

- Statistics layer - Hypothesis testing, causal inference, Bayesian methods
- Data layer - SQL, pandas, Spark, data wrangling at scale
- Modeling layer - scikit-learn, XGBoost, TensorFlow/PyTorch, feature engineering
- Experimentation layer - A/B testing, multi-armed bandits, uplift modeling
- Communication layer - Dashboards, presentations, stakeholder management


## Learning Path for Data Scientists

- Foundation - SQL, Python, statistics fundamentals (distributions, hypothesis testing, regression)
- Core ML - scikit-learn, classification, regression, clustering, evaluation metrics
- Experimentation - A/B testing, power analysis, causal inference methods
- Deep Learning - neural networks, NLP basics, when to use vs. simpler models
- Communication - data storytelling, dashboard design, stakeholder management
- Production - model deployment basics, monitoring, feature engineering at scale

For a structured learning path with real DS interview questions, statistical frameworks, and salary data, see [The 0→1 Data Scientist Interview Playbook](https://valenx.org/books/ds).
