# Behavioral Questions for Data Scientists

There are typically "tell me about a time you ..." questions.

Based on candidate reports from Reddit, Glassdoor, and personal blogs about what they were actually asked in data science interviews.


## Format

Typically 30-60 minutes, conversational. The interviewer picks a theme (conflict, leadership, failure, analytical decision-making) and goes deep on one or two scenarios rather than asking many surface-level questions.

Common formats:

- Single-scenario deep dive: the interviewer picks one situation from your experience and spends most of the round exploring it from every angle
- Category-based rotation: the interviewer covers 3-4 topics (conflict, leadership, failure, motivation) with one question each


## Common Behavioral Questions

### DS-Specific Behavioral

- Tell me about a time your analysis changed a product or business decision. What was the decision before your analysis? What changed after? [^glassdoor-google-ds]
- Describe a time when your data contradicted a stakeholder's assumption. How did you handle it? [^glassdoor-meta-ds]
- Tell me about a time you built a model or analysis that did not work as expected. What did you learn? [^glassdoor-netflix-ds]
- Walk me through the most impactful analysis you have done in the last year. Why did it matter? [^interviewquery-behavioral]
- Tell me about a time you had to prioritize between multiple analytical requests. How did you decide what to work on first? [^glassdoor-airbnb-ds]
- Describe a time when you had to explain a complex statistical concept to a non-technical audience. What approach did you use? [^glassdoor-uber-ds]
- Tell me about a time you identified a data quality issue that others had missed. What was the impact? [^glassdoor-spotify-ds]
- How do you stay up-to-date with the latest developments in data science and statistics? What have you learned recently? [^reddit-ds-behavioral]
- Tell me about a side project, Kaggle competition, or open-source contribution you are proud of [^interviewquery-behavioral]
- Describe a time when you had to work with incomplete or messy data. How did you handle the limitations? [^glassdoor-google-ds]
- Tell me about a time you designed an experiment from scratch. What challenges did you face? [^glassdoor-meta-ds]


### Cross-Functional Collaboration

These questions test your ability to work effectively with product managers, engineers, and business stakeholders - the core collaborative skill for data scientists.

- Tell me about a time you disagreed with a PM about the interpretation of data. How did you resolve it? [^glassdoor-meta-ds]
- Describe a time when you had to push back on a stakeholder who wanted to launch a feature based on insufficient data [^glassdoor-airbnb-ds]
- Tell me about a time you worked with an engineering team to deploy a model or data pipeline. What challenges came up? [^glassdoor-google-ds]
- How do you handle a situation where a stakeholder asks for an analysis that you believe is the wrong question to ask? [^glassdoor-netflix-ds]
- Tell me about a time you had to present findings to a senior executive. How did you adapt your communication? [^interviewquery-behavioral]
- Describe a time when you influenced a product decision through data, even though you did not have formal authority [^glassdoor-uber-ds]
- Tell me about a time you collaborated with another data scientist on a project. How did you divide the work? What did you learn from their approach? [^glassdoor-spotify-ds]
- Tell me about a time when different teams had conflicting interpretations of the same data. How did you help resolve the conflict? [^reddit-ds-behavioral]


### Leadership and Ownership

- Tell me about a time you identified an analytical opportunity that nobody asked for and drove it to completion [^glassdoor-google-ds]
- Describe a time when you mentored a junior analyst or data scientist. What was your approach? [^glassdoor-meta-ds]
- Tell me about a time you took ownership of improving a team process, dashboard, or analytical workflow [^glassdoor-airbnb-ds]
- How do you set priorities for your analytical work when there are more requests than time? [^glassdoor-netflix-ds]
- Tell me about a time you made a short-term sacrifice (speed, scope) for long-term analytical quality [^interviewquery-behavioral]
- Describe a project where you had to quickly learn a new domain or technique. How did you approach it? [^glassdoor-uber-ds]
- Tell me about a time you drove a decision that affected multiple teams [^glassdoor-spotify-ds]
- How do you handle disagreements with your manager about analytical priorities or methods? [^reddit-ds-behavioral]


### Failure and Learning

- Tell me about a time your analysis was wrong. What happened and what did you learn? [^glassdoor-google-ds]
- Describe a model you built that failed in production. What went wrong? [^glassdoor-meta-ds]
- Tell me about a time you made an incorrect recommendation based on data. How did you discover the error? [^glassdoor-netflix-ds]
- What is the biggest mistake you have made as a data scientist? What did you change as a result? [^interviewquery-behavioral]
- Tell me about a time when an experiment you designed did not give useful results. What would you do differently? [^glassdoor-airbnb-ds]
- Describe a time you received harsh feedback on your analysis. How did you respond? [^glassdoor-uber-ds]
- Tell me about a project that took much longer than expected. What caused the delay and how did you handle it? [^reddit-ds-behavioral]
- Why do you think we should not hire you? [^glassdoor-google-ds]


### Amazon Leadership Principles (ALP)

Amazon uses these principles explicitly in every behavioral round. Candidates preparing for Amazon DS roles should have one story per principle, with specific metrics.

- Customer Obsession: tell me about a time you dove deep into user data and discovered an insight that changed how the team served customers
- Bias for Action: tell me about a time you made an analytical decision with incomplete data. What was the outcome?
- Earn Trust: tell me about a time you built credibility with a skeptical stakeholder by showing your analysis was rigorous
- Think Big: tell me about a time you proposed an analytical framework or methodology that went beyond what was asked
- Deliver Results: tell me about a time you shipped an analysis under deadline pressure. How did you ensure quality?
- Disagree and Commit: tell me about a time you disagreed with a colleague's analytical approach, then committed to their method once the team decided
- Dive Deep: tell me about a time you found a root cause in data that others had missed by going deeper into the numbers
- Invent and Simplify: tell me about a time you automated or simplified an analytical workflow that was previously manual
- Have Backbone: tell me about a time you pushed back on a product decision because the data did not support it
- Learn and Be Curious: tell me about a time you taught yourself a new statistical method or tool to solve a problem

A current list is at: https://www.amazon.jobs/content/en/our-workplace/leadership-principles


### Google Googliness

Google values what they call Googliness, a cluster of traits that includes intellectual humility, comfort with ambiguity, and doing what is right even when it is harder. Common questions:

- Tell me about a time you changed your analytical conclusion based on new evidence
- Describe a time when you helped a colleague who was struggling with a data problem, without being asked
- How do you stay curious about your domain and analytical methods?
- Tell me about a time you made a decision that was right for users even when it was uncomfortable for the business
- Describe a time you navigated ambiguity in a project where the requirements kept changing


### Career Motivation

- Why data science? What draws you to this field over software engineering, product management, or research? [^glassdoor-google-ds]
- Why this company? Show evidence of due diligence: used the product, read their data science blog, identified analytical challenges specific to their business [^glassdoor-meta-ds]
- Where do you see yourself in 3-5 years? Do you want to go deeper into technical work (IC track) or move into management? [^interviewquery-behavioral]
- What type of data problems excite you most? Give a specific example [^glassdoor-netflix-ds]
- Why are you leaving your current role? [^glassdoor-airbnb-ds]
- What is one thing you would change about how data science is practiced at most companies? [^reddit-ds-behavioral]


## How to Prepare

Find the leadership principles or values of the company you are interviewing for. For each value, prepare 2-3 scenarios from your experience where you can tell a story demonstrating that value using the STAR format (Situation, Task, Action, Result).

Map your stories to common DS behavioral themes:

- Analytical impact: when your analysis directly changed a business decision and you can quantify the outcome
- Data quality: when you caught a data issue that would have led to a wrong conclusion
- Cross-functional influence: when you convinced a non-technical stakeholder through clear communication and rigorous analysis
- Experimentation: when you designed or analyzed an experiment with surprising or counterintuitive results
- Failure and recovery: when an analysis or model went wrong and what you learned
- Ownership: when you identified an analytical opportunity and drove it without being asked

If the company does not have publicly shared values, use Amazon's Leadership Principles as a starting point. Many companies have something similar.

DS-specific tips:

- Every story should include specific numbers: "I analyzed 2M user records and found that..." not "I analyzed a lot of data"
- Show the full analytical lifecycle: problem definition, data exploration, methodology choice, findings, recommendation, outcome
- Include at least one story about a time your analysis was wrong or inconclusive. This demonstrates intellectual honesty
- For senior roles, include stories about mentoring, setting analytical agendas, and influencing strategy

### Common mistakes

- Giving generic STAR answers without concrete specifics: numbers, outcomes, what actually happened
- Reusing the same story across multiple rounds instead of preparing distinct examples
- Talking about technical methods without connecting them to business outcomes
- Bluffing when you do not know something instead of being honest and curious
- Framing everything as a solo effort: data scientists work cross-functionally. Show how you worked with PMs, engineers, and business stakeholders
- Not preparing DS-specific examples: make sure at least two stories demonstrate analytical rigor and data-driven decision making explicitly
