# From Business / MBA to Data Scientist

Business professionals bring domain expertise, stakeholder management, and strategic thinking. The transition to data science requires building a technical foundation in Python, statistics, and machine learning. The learning curve is steeper than other paths but the combination of business acumen with technical skills is highly valued.


## What You Already Have

- Domain expertise and business context
- Stakeholder management and executive communication
- Strategic thinking and problem framing
- Financial modeling and business case development
- Cross-functional team leadership
- Understanding of key business metrics and KPIs


## What You Need to Learn

- Python programming - from scratch through pandas, NumPy, scikit-learn
- SQL - querying databases, building analytical queries, joins, aggregations
- Statistics fundamentals - distributions, hypothesis testing, regression, confidence intervals
- Machine learning - classification, regression, clustering, model evaluation
- Data visualization - matplotlib, seaborn, Tableau for different audiences
- Experimental design - A/B testing, sample sizes, statistical significance
- Version control - Git basics for collaborative work


## Why This Transition Works

The business side is the hardest part for most technical data scientists to learn. You already have it. You understand why a 2% increase in retention matters, how to frame a model's output as a business decision, and how to communicate results to executives. These skills take years to develop organically.

The technical gap is real but manageable. Python, SQL, and statistics are learnable skills with clear curricula. ML fundamentals can be learned in weeks with the right resources.


## Suggested Path

1. Learn Python basics - data types, loops, functions. Use a beginner course (Codecademy, DataCamp)
2. Learn SQL - start querying immediately, practice on real datasets
3. Build statistics fundamentals - hypothesis testing, regression, distributions
4. Learn pandas and data manipulation - cleaning, reshaping, aggregating data
5. Start machine learning - scikit-learn classification and regression
6. Build end-to-end projects - take a business question through the full data science workflow
7. Apply your business skills - frame every project in terms of business impact


## Timeline

4-6 months of focused learning. The technical ramp is longer because you are building programming and statistics skills from a lower base. But your business context and communication skills give you an immediate advantage once the technical skills are in place.


## Your Advantage

You can connect data science work to business outcomes. When a data scientist says "the model has 0.85 AUC," you can translate that into "this saves $2M annually in fraud losses." That translation skill is rare and valuable, especially at companies where data science teams struggle to demonstrate business impact.

For a structured approach to DS interviews, check out [The 0→1 Data Scientist Interview Playbook](https://valenx.org/books/ds).
