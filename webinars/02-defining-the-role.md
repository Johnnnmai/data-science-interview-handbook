# Webinar 2: Defining the Data Scientist Role

- Date: February 24, 2026
- Host: [Alexey Grigorev](https://www.linkedin.com/in/agrigorev/)

## Description

Data science job titles are inconsistent. This webinar examines 1,350+ current job descriptions and industry research to clarify what companies actually hire for, helping professionals distinguish between analytics-focused, modeling-focused, and research-oriented DS positions.

## Topics Covered

- Data analysis: scraping and processing job descriptions using LLMs to identify patterns
- Role classification: distinguishing between analytics-first, modeling-first, and research DS positions
- Technical skills: SQL, Python, statistics, and ML requirements by role type
- Experimentation: A/B testing and causal inference as core competencies
- Production focus: shipping models versus generating insights
- Career strategy: actionable approaches for securing DS positions
- Industry trends: interview patterns and evolving skill expectations

## Key Findings

### Dataset

The analysis is based on approximately 1,350+ job descriptions scraped from major job boards in early 2026. Jobs were collected from multiple cities. Each description was processed using an LLM to extract structured information: company details, position type (analytics-first, modeling-first, or research DS), responsibilities, use cases, and skills categorized by type.

### Skills

72.4% of positions were analytics or product-focused roles. The remaining were modeling-first or research roles.

Top skills from job descriptions:

- Python is the dominant language (94.7%), SQL in second place (91.2%)
- Statistics and hypothesis testing appear in 57.9% of roles
- Machine learning is mentioned in 66.0% of roles
- Experimentation and A/B testing appear in 52.7% of roles
- Communication skills are explicitly mentioned in 39.6% of roles
- Deep learning appears in only 31.3% of roles - most DS work uses simpler models

The key takeaway: a data scientist is first an analytical thinker, then a technical specialist. Just knowing how to train models is not enough - you also need SQL fluency, experimental design, and communication skills.

### Responsibilities

Very common: data analysis and insight generation, experimentation design, metric definition, stakeholder communication, building dashboards and reports.

Common: predictive modeling, feature engineering, cross-functional collaboration, customer analytics.

Less common: deep learning, NLP, production ML infrastructure.


## Q&A Highlights

### Data scientist with strong analytics transitioning to ML roles

Data scientists with analytics backgrounds are well equipped for ML roles. The mindset of hypothesis testing and data-driven decisions is exactly what modeling requires. Start by adding scikit-learn and feature engineering to your toolkit.

### Are data science jobs disappearing?

No. From studies analyzing millions of job postings across years, data science is one of the top growing roles. Companies need people who can turn data into decisions - that need is increasing, not decreasing.

### Top 3 must-have skills for DS interviews?

1. SQL fluency - used daily and tested in every interview
2. Statistics fundamentals - hypothesis testing, regression, experimental design
3. Python with pandas and scikit-learn - for both analysis and modeling
