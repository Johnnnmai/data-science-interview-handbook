# Webinar 1: A Day in the Life of a Data Scientist

- Date: February 16, 2026
- Host: [Alexey Grigorev](https://www.linkedin.com/in/agrigorev/)

## Description

The role of a Data Scientist is often misunderstood as purely model-building or Kaggle-style work. This session cuts through theory to show the practical reality of the role.

## Topics Covered

- Defining the Data Scientist from a practitioner perspective
- Core responsibilities: turning data into business decisions
- SQL and analytics as the daily foundation
- Experimentation design and A/B testing workflows
- Production concerns: model monitoring, data quality, and drift detection
- Essential skills for modern Data Scientists
- Q&A session

## Key Takeaways

1. Define the DS Role - understanding core responsibilities bridging statistical analysis and business impact
2. Collaborate with Others - how data scientists work within cross-functional teams
3. Processes - applying structured frameworks to experimental design and metric definition


## Q&A Highlights

### How can a data analyst transition to become a data scientist?

For data analysts, data science is primarily about adding statistical depth and modeling skills on top of what you already know. Most of the daily work overlaps - SQL, dashboards, stakeholder communication. The difference is in the depth of analysis and the ability to build predictive models.

The specific skills to learn: statistics beyond descriptive analytics, Python for modeling, experimental design. After 2-3 months of focused learning, a data analyst should be ready to transition.

### What backgrounds make good data scientists?

Engineers and analysts are excellent future data scientists. For analysts, it is probably the easiest transition - you already understand the data and the business context. For engineers, you bring production skills that most data scientists lack.

PhD researchers also transition well - their statistical rigor is a permanent advantage. The gap is business framing and communication speed.

### Is the Data Scientist role becoming automated?

Not the core skills. Basic reporting and dashboard creation are increasingly automated. But experimental design, causal inference, business framing, and communicating uncertain results to stakeholders - these require human judgment and are not going away.

Full-stack data scientists who can translate analysis into business decisions have never had problems finding work and likely never will.
