# Webinar 3: The Data Science Interview Process

- Date: March 3, 2026
- Host: [Alexey Grigorev](https://www.linkedin.com/in/agrigorev/)

## Description

The DS hiring landscape blends statistical reasoning with coding skills and business thinking. By examining real candidate experiences from Reddit and X, this session prepares participants for the DS screening reality.

For a comprehensive breakdown of DS interview formats, question banks, and preparation strategies, see [The 0→1 Data Scientist Interview Playbook](https://valenx.org/books/ds).

## Topics Covered

- Interview process structure - the 7 typical steps from recruiter screen to final round
- Statistics questions - probability, hypothesis testing, experimental design, causal inference
- SQL and coding rounds - two types: practical queries vs. algorithmic coding
- Project deep dive - how hiring managers probe your analytical work for depth and rigor
- ML concepts - model selection, evaluation, feature engineering, trade-offs
- Behavioral interviews - preparing stories using company values
- Take-home analysis projects - preview of patterns from candidate reports

## Key Findings

### Interview Process Structure

From job descriptions and candidate reports, the typical DS interview has up to 7 steps:

1. CV screening
2. Recruiter call - who are you, what do you know about us, salary expectations
3. Statistics and probability round - conceptual and applied questions
4. SQL and coding round - practical queries and Python coding
5. Case study or product analytics round - business problem solving with data
6. Behavioral interview - sometimes separate, sometimes merged
7. Take-home analysis project + defence round - present and defend your analysis

### Statistics Questions

Core topics: probability, distributions, hypothesis testing, regression assumptions, Bayesian reasoning, experimental design. The best preparation is through practice and understanding, not memorization.

### SQL and Coding Rounds

Two types:

- Practical SQL rounds - write queries to answer business questions (cohort analysis, funnel metrics)
- Python coding rounds - implement algorithms, data manipulation with pandas, statistical computations

### Project Deep Dive

The hiring manager picks one project from your experience and goes deep for 30 minutes. They want to understand your analytical methodology, statistical choices, business impact, and lessons learned.
