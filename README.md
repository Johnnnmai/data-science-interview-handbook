## The Data Scientist Role

What data scientists actually do, based on 1,350+ job descriptions.

| Guide | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| [My vision of the role](role/01-my-vision.md) | How I see data science, DS vs MLE vs DA vs Analytics Engineer, the Research-to-Production lifecycle |
| [Skills analysis](role/02-skills.md) | Top DS skills, statistical methods, ML frameworks, tools like pandas and scikit-learn |
| [Responsibilities](role/03-responsibilities.md) | Patterns extracted from 4,200+ job responsibilities |
| [Use cases](role/04-use-cases.md) | Real data science scenarios showing what companies build and ship |
| [Reality vs. job postings](role/05-reality-vs-postings.md) | What candidates experience vs. what is advertised |

87.3% of DS roles require skills beyond statistical modeling. SQL is mandatory in 91.2% of roles. Only 5.1% are pure research - the market wants people who ship production models and drive business decisions.

## Career Transitions

5 paths into data science, each with timeline, what transfers, and what to learn.

| From | Timeline | Your Superpower |
|------|----------|----------------|
| [Data Analyst](learning-paths/from-analyst.md) | 2-3 months | SQL fluency, business context, dashboard experience |
| [Software Engineer](learning-paths/from-software-engineer.md) | 3-4 months | Production code, system design, deployment |
| [Academia / PhD](learning-paths/from-academia.md) | 2-4 months | Statistical rigor, research methodology, deep ML knowledge |
| [Business / MBA](learning-paths/from-business.md) | 4-6 months | Strategy, stakeholder management, domain expertise |
| [Self-Taught](learning-paths/from-self-taught.md) | 6-9 months | Motivation, diverse perspective, project portfolio |
